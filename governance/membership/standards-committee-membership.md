# Standards Committee Membership

## 1. Introduction

The Standards Committee ensures consistency, quality, and alignment across engineering standards. This document outlines the structure, membership types, and requirements for participation in the Standards Committee, ensuring a fair and transparent process for selecting members.

---

## 2. Membership Structure

### 2.1 Permanent Members
Permanent Members are core participants who provide consistent leadership and decision-making within the Standards Committee. They are responsible for:
- Representing their respective engineering domains.
- Ensuring alignment with organizational goals and standards.
- Mentoring Temporary Members and fostering collaboration.

### 2.2 Temporary Members
Temporary Members are invited to participate in the Standards Committee on a case-by-case basis to provide expertise on specific topics or projects. Their involvement is time-limited and focused on:
- Contributing specialized knowledge.
- Supporting decision-making for specific initiatives.

### 2.3 Facilitator
The Facilitator, typically a Manager from the Engineering team, ensures that Standards Committee meetings are productive and that decisions are documented and communicated effectively. The Facilitator is not a voting member but plays a critical role in:
- Scheduling and organizing meetings.
- Ensuring adherence to governance processes.
- Documenting decisions and action items.
- Communicating outcomes to relevant stakeholders.

---

## 3. Membership Types: Roles and Responsibilities

### Permanent Members
- Serve as long-term representatives of their engineering domains.
- Provide strategic guidance and ensure continuity in standards-related decisions.
- Nominate and mentor Temporary Members as needed.

### Temporary Members
- Participate in discussions relevant to their expertise.
- Provide input on specific projects or initiatives.
- Step down once their contributions are complete.

### Facilitator
- Ensure that meetings are productive and well-structured.
- Document decisions and action items.
- Communicate outcomes to relevant stakeholders.

---

## 4. Membership Requirements

To ensure the Standards Committee is composed of qualified and committed individuals, the following criteria apply:

- **Eligibility**: Members must hold an Engineer Level 4 role or above within the organization.
- **Nomination**: Candidates must be nominated by their department Director and approved by the Standards Committee Sponsors.
- **Commitment**: Members must dedicate sufficient time to actively participate in Standards Committee activities and decisions.

---

## 5. Committee Size and Rotation

To maintain effectiveness and ensure diverse perspectives, the Standards Committee is intentionally kept small, with:
- **Permanent Members**: Ideally 4–5 individuals, ensuring manageable discussions and decision-making.
- **Rotation**: Permanent Members may rotate periodically to provide opportunities for other qualified individuals while maintaining continuity.
- **Continuity**: Overlapping terms for Permanent Members ensure that institutional knowledge is preserved.

---

This structure ensures that the Standards Committee remains agile, inclusive, and aligned with the organization’s evolving needs.
