# **Service Level Agreements (SLAs)**

This document defines the Service Level Agreements (SLAs) for governance processes within the Engineering Governance Framework. SLAs ensure timely reviews, decisions, and updates to maintain efficiency and accountability.

---

### **SLAs for Governance Processes**

#### **1. RFC Review SLA**
- **Purpose**: Ensure timely feedback on Requests for Comment (RFCs).
- **Timeframe**: RFCs must be reviewed within **5 business days** of submission.
- **Responsibility**: Standards Committee or ARB, depending on the RFC's scope.
- **Escalation**: If no feedback is provided within the SLA, escalate to the facilitator.

#### **2. ADR Lightweight Review SLA**
- **Purpose**: Ensure low-risk architectural decisions are reviewed promptly.
- **Timeframe**: ADRs must be reviewed within **5 business days** of submission.
- **Responsibility**: ARB members.
- **Process**: If no objections are raised within the SLA, the ADR is **auto-approved (lazy consensus)**.
- **Escalation**: If no decision is made within the SLA, escalate to the ARB facilitator.

#### **3. ADR Full Review SLA**
- **Purpose**: Ensure high-impact architectural decisions are reviewed thoroughly.
- **Timeframe**: ADRs must be reviewed within **10 business days** of submission.
- **Responsibility**: ARB members.
- **Escalation**: If no decision is made within the SLA, escalate to the ARB facilitator.

#### **4. Standards Update SLA**
- **Purpose**: Ensure updates to Standards are reviewed and published promptly.
- **Timeframe**: Proposed updates must be reviewed within **10 business days** of submission.
- **Responsibility**: Standards Committee members.
- **Escalation**: If no action is taken within the SLA, escalate to the facilitator.

#### **5. Artifact Lifecycle SLA**
- **Purpose**: Ensure artifacts (RFCs, ADRs, Standards) progress through their lifecycle stages without delays.
- **Timeframe**:
  - **Draft to Review**: Transition within **5 business days**.
  - **Review to Final Decision**: Transition within **10 business days**.
- **Responsibility**: Artifact owners and relevant governance bodies.
- **Escalation**: Escalate to the respective governance body facilitator if delays occur.

#### **6. ARB Triage SLA**
- **Purpose**: Ensure ADRs are triaged promptly to determine the appropriate review path.
- **Timeframe**: ADRs must be triaged within **2 business days** of submission.
- **Responsibility**: ARB Facilitator or designated reviewer.
- **Escalation**: If triage is not completed within the SLA, escalate to ARB member.

---

### **SLA Compliance and Monitoring**
- **Tracking**: Use GitHub Issues or a similar tool to track SLA compliance.
- **Reporting**: Generate monthly reports on SLA adherence and identify bottlenecks.
- **Continuous Improvement**: Regularly review SLAs and adjust timeframes or processes as needed.

---

### **How to Propose SLA Changes**
1. Submit an RFC outlining the proposed changes.
2. Include rationale, impact analysis, and updated timeframes.
3. Obtain approval from the relevant governance body.
