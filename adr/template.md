# 1. Summary
Describe the forces at play that led to this decision. Include:
- Technical constraints  
- Organizational or political factors  
- Security or compliance requirements  
- Operational realities  
- Conflicting priorities or tensions  

This section is **value‑neutral**. It should describe the situation, not advocate for a solution.

---

# 2. Decision
State the architectural decision clearly and unambiguously.

Use active voice, e.g.:
- “We will migrate…”
- “We will adopt…”
- “We will deprecate…”
- “We will standardize on…”

This section should stand on its own as the authoritative statement of the decision.

---

# 3. Scope
Define what this decision **does** and **does not** affect.

### In Scope
- Systems, teams, platforms, or domains impacted  
- Environments (prod, non‑prod, cloud providers, etc.)  

### Out of Scope
- Areas explicitly not affected  
- Future phases or follow‑on work  

---

# 4. Consequences
List all consequences — positive, negative, and neutral.

Examples:
- Benefits  
- Risks  
- Required migrations  
- Operational impacts  
- Cost implications  
- Tooling or process changes  
- Training or documentation needs  

Be explicit. Future teams will rely on this section to understand the tradeoffs.

# 6. Conventions
